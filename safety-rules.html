FIS Alpine Skiing Code — 10 Rules

1

Mutual Respect

Skiers and snowboarders must behave in a way that does not endanger or prejudice others or their equipment.

2

Speed Control

Adapt speed and manner of skiing to personal ability, terrain conditions, snow conditions, weather, and density of people on the slope.

3

Route Selection

The skier/snowboarder coming from behind must choose a route that ensures the safety of those ahead.

4

Overtaking

Overtaking is allowed from above or below, from right or left, but always with sufficient space for the person being overtaken. The overtaker is responsible throughout.

5

Entry & Starting

Every skier/snowboarder entering a marked run or starting after stopping must look both up and down the slope and ensure it is safe to do so.

6

Slope Stopping

Avoid stopping in narrow places or where visibility is limited. After a fall, clear the slope as quickly as possible.

7

Walking on Slopes

Stay to the edge of the slope when walking up or down. Footprints create hazards for other users.

8

Sign Compliance

Follow all slope markings and signs. Slopes are rated by difficulty: green (beginner), blue (intermediate), red (advanced), black (expert).

9

Accident Assistance

In case of accident, every skier/snowboarder is duty bound to assist. Secure the accident area and alert rescue services.

10

Information Exchange

All persons involved in an accident, including witnesses, must exchange personal details and contact information.

General Resort Rules

Children must not be left unattended on resort territory at any time.

Littering and leaving waste outside designated areas is strictly prohibited.

Unauthorized commercial filming or photography for commercial purposes requires prior approval.

Alcohol consumption outside of designated restaurant and bar areas is not permitted.

Smoking is only allowed in designated smoking areas.

Drone use on resort territory requires advance written permission from resort management.

Suspicious items or packages must be reported to resort security immediately.

Ski Pass Rules

Ski passes are magnetic cards granting access to lifts, prepared slopes, and storage lockers.

Passes are strictly non-transferable. A second use of a single-use pass will result in non-refundable cancellation.

Counterfeiting or duplication of passes will result in immediate involvement of law enforcement authorities.

Cable Car Rules

Children: Children under 130 cm require adult supervision on chairlifts. Children under 1 year are prohibited on chairlifts.

Gondolas: Maximum 8 passengers per cabin. Remain seated during travel. Do not obstruct doors, open doors while moving, throw items, or rock cabins.

Chairlifts: Skis must be fastened; snowboards on one foot only. Do not swing chairs or raise safety bars while in motion.

Conveyor Lifts: Board only at the designated start point. Maintain a forward-facing position. Do not sit, move on the belt, or exit before the designated exit point.

Tubing Rules

Access restrictions: Children under 130 cm must be accompanied by an adult. Children under 4 years require direct supervision. Prohibited for persons with heart conditions, pregnant women, and those who are intoxicated.

Footwear: Heel shoes and ski boots are prohibited on tubing slopes.

Operation: Sit upright only. No flipping or rocking tubes. Spinning, hand-braking, and linked descents are forbidden.

Equipment: Return tubes undamaged. Damage liability applies on the same day of rental.

Ski Slope Rules

The skier/snowboarder downslope always has right of way. Those coming from behind must ensure safe distance.

Adapt speed to personal skill level, visibility, slope density, and weather conditions at all times.

Helmets are mandatory for all ages on all slopes and lifts.

Skiing or snowboarding while intoxicated is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate pass cancellation.

Violations of slope rules may result in pass cancellation and blacklisting from the resort.

Locker Rules

Storage of animals, weapons, explosives, or hazardous materials in lockers is strictly prohibited.

Report lost locker keys to resort staff immediately.

The resort is not responsible for the security of personal property left in lockers.

Equipment Rental Rules

Valid ID and a signed rental contract are required before equipment is issued.

Minimum rental period is one day. Fees are non-refundable after first use.

Renting equipment while intoxicated, subletting, self-repairing, removing from resort property, or unauthorized transfers are all prohibited.

Clients are financially responsible for any damage or loss of rented equipment.

All equipment must be returned clean at least 15 minutes before resort closing time.

Instructor Rules

Instructor services do not include lift passes or equipment rental — these must be purchased separately.

Lessons while intoxicated or ignoring instructor guidance are prohibited.

Informal or unauthorized hiring of resort instructors is not permitted.

Unused prepaid lesson time is non-refundable.

Parking Rules

Parking facilities operate 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Vehicles with fuel/oil leaks, missing registration, or carrying hazardous cargo are not permitted.

Maximum speed on parking territory is 5 km/hour.

Vehicle repairs, washing, open fires, and extended overnight stays are prohibited.

The resort is not responsible for theft or damage to vehicles or their contents.
